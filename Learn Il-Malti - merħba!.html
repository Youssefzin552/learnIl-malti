      Emergency Phrases in Maltese


      General Emergencies

      • Għinni! - Help me!
      • Hemm emerġenza! - There's an emergency!
      • Ċempel għall-għajnuna! - Call for help!

      Medical Emergencies

      • Jien ferut/a. - I'm injured.
      • Jien morda/a. - I'm sick.
      • Ħadd m'hu qed iwieġeb. - No one is answering.
      • Jien allerġiku/a għal... - I'm allergic to...
      • Jien qed niddiżorjenta. - I'm disoriented.

      Specific Emergencies

      • Hemm nar! - There's a fire!
      • Hemm serq! - There's a robbery!
      • Hemm attentat ta' suwiċidju! - There's a suicide attempt!
      • Il-karozza ħabtet! - The car crashed!
      • Hemm xi ħadd maqbud! - Someone is trapped!

      Asking for Help

      • Fejn hu l-eqreb sptar? - Where's the nearest hospital?
      • Fejn hu l-eqreb stazzjoni tal-pulizija? - Where's the nearest police station?
      • Fejn hu l-eqreb stazzjoni tat-tifi tan-nar? - Where's the nearest fire station?
      • Fejn hu l-eqreb ambulatorju? - Where's the nearest clinic?

      Going to a Restaurant

      Phrases that can be used when going to a restaurant

      • Can I have the menu, please? - Tista' tagħtini l-menu, jekk jogħġbok?
      • I'd like to order... - Nixtieq niordna...
      • Is that gluten-free? - Huwa mingħajr glutine?
      • Can I have a glass of water, please? - Tista' tagħtini tazza ilma, jekk jogħġbok?

      • Asking for Recommendations

        • Can you recommend a good wine to go with this dish? - Tista' tirrakkomandali flixkun tajjeb ta' vin biex imur ma' din id-dixx?

      • Paying the Bill

        • Can I have the bill, please? - Tista' tagħtini l-kont, jekk jogħġbok?
        • Do you accept credit cards? - Taċċettaw karti tal-kreditu?

      • Compliments and Complaints

        • This is delicious! - Dan huwa delizzjuż!
        • Excuse me, there seems to be a mistake on my bill. - Skużani, jidher li hemm żball fil-kont tiegħi.

      Maltese Terms for Marital Status

      • Miżżewġin: Married
      • Divorzjati: Divorced
      • Widwi/Widwa: Widowed
      • Fidanzati: Engaged
      • Single: Not married (this term is used directly from English)

      Maltese Expressions Using Marital Status Terms

      Married:

      • Huma miżżewġin minn għoxrin sena. (They have been married for twenty years.)
      • Qed niċċelebraw l-anniversarju taż-żwieġ tagħna. (We are celebrating our wedding anniversary.)

      Divorced:

      • Huma divorzjati minn tliet snin. (They have been divorced for three years.)
      • Hija divorzjata b'tliet ulied. (She is divorced with three children.)

      Widowed:

      • Huwa widwi minn sena. (He has been widowed for a year.)
      • Hija widwa u għandha żewġ ibniet. (She is widowed and has two daughters.)

      Engaged:

      • Huma fidanzati minn sitt xhur. (They have been engaged for six months.)
      • Qed nippjanaw il-tieġ tagħna. (We are planning our wedding.)

      Single:

      • Hija single u tgawdi l-ħajja tagħha. (She is single and enjoying her life.)
      • Huwa single u qed ifittex relazzjoni. (He is single and looking for a relationship.)

      Drinks and Food in Maltese

      Food

      • Ħobż (hobz): Bread, typically a round, crusty loaf
      • Pastizzi (pas-tiz-zi): Savory pastries, usually filled with ricotta cheese or peas
      • Lampuki (lam-pu-ki): Dorado fish, a popular dish in Malta
      • Fenkata (fen-ka-ta): Rabbit stew, a traditional Maltese meal
      • Għaġin (gha-gin): Pasta, often served with a tomato sauce

      Drinks

      • Biera (be-ra): Beer
      • Inbid (in-bid): Wine
      • Kafè (ka-feh): Coffee
      • Te (teh): Tea
      • Ilma (il-ma): Water
      • Limunata (li-mu-na-ta): Lemonade

      Maltese Phrases For begginers

      Learn essential Maltese phrases for beginners in this helpful blog post.

      Greetings and Farewells

      • Hello: "Bonġu" (pronounced "bon-joo")
      • Goodbye: "Arrivederci" (pronounced "ah-ree-vehr-deh-chee")
      • How are you? "Kif int?" (pronounced "kif int")
      • I'm fine, thank you: "Tajjeb, grazzi" (pronounced "taj-eb, grazzi")

      Basic Phrases

      • Yes: "Iva" (pronounced "ee-va")
      • No: "Le" (pronounced "leh")
      • Please: "Jekk jogħġbok" (pronounced "jekk yo-gh-bok")
      • Thank you: "Grazzi" (pronounced "grazzi")
      • You're welcome: "Xejn" (pronounced "shehn")

      Asking for Directions

      • Where is the bathroom? "Fejn hu t-twalett?" (pronounced "fehn hu t-twal-ett")
      • Where is the restaurant? "Fejn hu r-ristorant?" (pronounced "fehn hu r-ris-to-rant")

      Numbers

      • One: "Wieħed" (pronounced "wee-hed")
      • Two: "Tnejn" (pronounced "tnejn")
      • Three: "Tlieta" (pronounced "tlee-ta")
      • Four: "Erba' (pronounced "er-bah")
      • Five: "Ħamsa" (pronounced "ham-sa")
